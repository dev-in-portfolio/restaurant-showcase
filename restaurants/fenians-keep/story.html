Our Story

A neighborhood public house built on rich Irish tradition, spirits, and community.

Fenian's Keep opened its doors in NoDa in March 2026 with a simple mission: to serve as the neighborhood's cozy living room. No food. No pretension. Just great drinks, warm company, and a place to rest your head at the end of a long day.

Owner Kyle Musumeci, who also runs Brigid's Irish Pub in South End, brought the same community-first philosophy north to NoDa. "My main focus has always been integration into the local neighborhood," Musumeci says. "Those are the watering holes I love going to. A local spot where everyone knows you and everyone knows everyone else — that's the community spirit you search for in a great pub."

The name Fenian's Keep honors the rich history of Irish hospitality — a "keep" representing a fortified stronghold or gathering place, and "Fenian" rooted in the ancient Irish warrior bands, the Fianna. The name evokes strength, brotherhood, and welcoming hospitality. Like the historic keeps of Ireland, we stand as a place of refuge, warmth, and genuine belonging.

"There is no stranger at an Irish pub — only friends you haven't met yet."

— Traditional Irish Toast

From the beginning, Fenian's Keep has focused exclusively on what it does best: pours and spirits. Our back bar features a carefully selected list of Irish whiskeys, from familiar staples like Jameson to single pot still gems like Redbreast 12 Year and Green Spot. Our draft lines pour Guinness dry stout exactly the way it's meant to be served, alongside rotating Charlotte craft beers and European imports. For mixed drink lovers, our cocktail menu applies a proper Irish spin to classic mixes, including the copper-mugged Irish Mule and our house signature Irish Coffee.

With screens for match days, live music nights, and a classic jukebox setting the tone, Fenian's Keep is designed to fit the rhythm of NoDa — a comfortable stop on your night out, or the only stop you'll need. Stop by for our daily drink specials and raise a glass with us.
