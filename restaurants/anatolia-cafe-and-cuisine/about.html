Our Story

A journey from Istanbul to Charlotte, through the heart of Anatolia.

Travertine & Hearth

Named After an Ancient Land

Anatolia Cafe & Cuisine takes its name from Anatolia — the historic peninsula that is home to modern-day Turkey. It is a land where cultures, flavors, and traditions have merged for millennia, and that spirit of fusion is exactly what we bring to University City, Charlotte.

Opened on April 18, 2025, Anatolia is a Turkish and Mediterranean "hybrid house" — part cafe, part patisserie, part kebab house. We serve everything from in-house baked borek and baklava to flame-grilled Adana kebabs and Moroccan herbal teas.

Founding Family

Mehmet Tarhan

Mehmet Tarhan had a vision — to create a space in Charlotte where Turkish and Mediterranean food could be experienced authentically, not as a compromise. After years of planning, he brought Anatolia to life in the heart of University City.

Tarhan's wife is Moroccan, and her influence can be found throughout the cafe — particularly in our curated selection of Moroccan herbal teas, which sit alongside our Turkish coffee program as a tribute to both families' traditions.

Master Baker & Griller

Chef Omer Aydin

Chef Omer Aydin brings 15 years of professional culinary experience to Anatolia, including years spent operating two of his own stores in Istanbul, Turkey. His expertise spans the full breadth of Turkish cuisine — from the delicate art of pastry-making to the bold, smoky flavors of flame-grilled kebabs.

At Anatolia, Chef Omer oversees everything that comes out of our kitchen. He is deeply committed to authenticity — every borek is hand-rolled, every simit is shaped by hand, and every kebab is seasoned and grilled the way it would be in Istanbul.

Visuals & Spaces

A Taste of Turkey, Inside and Out

Step inside Anatolia and you'll find yourself surrounded by beautiful artwork and decor highlighting different regions of Turkey — from the coastal beauty of the Aegean to the ancient ruins of Cappadocia, from the bustling bazaars of Istanbul to the quiet villages of the Black Sea.

Community Seat

The "Hybrid House"

Anatolia is what we call a "hybrid house" — a space that blends the warmth of a bakery-cafe with the boldness of a kebab restaurant. You can grab a pistachio baklava and a Turkish coffee for breakfast, come back for a quick borek lunch, and return in the evening for Adana kebabs and mixed grills.

Our Core Beliefs

What We Stand For

Authenticity

Every recipe, technique, and ingredient is rooted in real Turkish and Mediterranean culinary tradition. No shortcuts.

In-House

Our borek, simit, baklava, and kunefe are baked fresh every morning. We believe in doing things the hard way — the right way.

Hospitality

Anatolian hospitality is legendary. Every guest is treated like family — with warmth, generosity, and genuine care.

Fusion

Turkish and Moroccan traditions come together in our menu and our space — reflecting the Tarhan family's own story.
