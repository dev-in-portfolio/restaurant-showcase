Our Story

About Caswell Station

The big house on the corner of Pecan and Caswell

Caswell Station started with a house. A big, beautiful, Elizabeth-neighborhood craftsman house built in 1913 on the corner of Pecan and Caswell. Rather than let it become just another development story, we saw what it could be: a place where the neighborhood could gather, eat, drink, and watch the game.

The Neighborhood

Elizabeth is one of Charlotte's oldest residential neighborhoods, and Caswell Station is built into its fabric. We're the place you walk to on a Saturday morning for brunch, the spot you hit after work on a Friday, and the corner where you run into your neighbors.

The Food

Our menu is New American — creative, sharable, and unpretentious. Tatchos (tater tots topped with short ribs and gravy), cheesesteak egg rolls, wings in four signature sauces, and street tacos. We also do a weekend brunch that the neighborhood takes seriously.

The Vibe

Casual. Loud. Fun. We've got TVs for every game, a garden patio for warm Carolina nights, live songwriter music on select evenings, and a friendly bar team that keeps things moving. We are your local tavern.
