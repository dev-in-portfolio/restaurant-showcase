OUR PHILOSOPHY

Our Story

A pasta bar born from the garden and cooked at the wood-fired open hearth.

From the Ground to the Hearth

Opened in 2018, Flour Shop has established itself as Charlotte's cozy neighborhood pasta sanctuary. Nestled in the Backlot of Myers Park's Park Road Shopping Center, Chef Trey Wilson constructed a rare, chef-centric space: a 24-seat U-shaped walnut bar wrapping around an open, wood-fired hearth.

Our philosophy is rooted in scratch-made simplicity. Everything from our breads and dessert reductions to our extruded and hand-stamped pasta shapes is made in-house daily. We source our fish and meats from sustainable Carolina producers, supplemented by herbs and greens harvested directly from Chef Trey's backyard garden plot.

This isn't farm-to-table as a buzzword; it's the core method of how our kitchen creates every single plate.

The U-Shaped Bar

24 stools wrapping around the open kitchen hearth. Watch dough rolled and cut, then roasted over oak wood fire right before you.

Scratch Prepared

Fresh flour, farm eggs, house-made stocks. If it can be hand-kneaded or fresh-whipped in our room, it is.

Chef Trey Wilson

Honoring seasonal ingredients with rustic technique.

Chef Trey's culinary approach is defined by a deep respect for seasonal produce and wood-fire cookery. By bridging local Carolina sourcing with hand-rolled pasta, he delivers dishes that are comforting yet refined. Flour Shop has earned its reputation as one of Charlotte's premier dining rooms, recognized by publications like Southern Living and maintaining a multi-year OpenTable Diners' Choice standing.
