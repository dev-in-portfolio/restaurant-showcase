Our Story

From Srebrenica to Central Avenue. A family's journey, a community's kitchen.

Srebrenica

In 1995, during the heights of the Bosnian War, Dino Mehic's father was lost in the Srebrenica genocide. Dino, his mother, and siblings survived. Three years later, in 1998, they immigrated to the United States as war refugees, settling in Charlotte's Central Avenue corridor—where many Balkan families gathered to build anew.

Bosna Market

Amela worked warehouse jobs while Dino stayed home to raise their children, Mehmed and Minela. In 2003, they opened Bosna Market on Central Avenue to support the growing Balkan refugee community—importing traditional smoked meats, regional cheeses, sodas, and spices from Bosnia, Croatia, Serbia, and across Europe. It quickly became a vital community space.

Euro Grill & Cafe

When Bosna Market expanded to its current location at 2719 Central Avenue, Dino and Amela decided to bring hot, fresh Bosnian food to Charlotte. They opened a doorway through the grocery market wall, leading down into a small, welcoming dining room. Euro Grill & Cafe was born.

Today, Dino runs the front of house with his signature warm humor. He'll welcome you, guide you through traditional Bosnian coffee rituals, and remind you to linger. The kitchen prepares everything to order, including hand-scored lepinja flatbreads and slow-grilled cevapi sausages. During busy weekends, family friends and Bosnian-trained chefs help maintain their heritage recipes.
